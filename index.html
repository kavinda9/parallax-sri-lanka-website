Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and palace ruins located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. This UNESCO World Heritage Site stands 200 meters high and features remarkable frescoes, landscaped gardens, and the famous Lion Gate. Built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century, it represents one of the best-preserved examples of ancient urban planning.

Discover Sri Lanka's Marine Life

Where the Ocean Meets Paradise

The Indian Ocean Surrounding Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, the pearl of the Indian Ocean, is surrounded by pristine waters teeming with vibrant marine life. The island's coastal waters are home to diverse ecosystems including coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds. These waters host numerous species including blue whales, dolphins, sea turtles, and countless tropical fish.

The convergence of warm tropical waters and nutrient-rich currents creates one of the world's most biodiverse marine environments. From the shallow reefs perfect for snorkeling to deep-sea diving spots, Sri Lanka offers unparalleled underwater experiences. The island's strategic location along ancient maritime trade routes has also resulted in fascinating underwater archaeological sites waiting to be explored.


Marine Conservation in Sri Lanka

    Sri Lanka is committed to protecting its marine heritage through various conservation efforts:

  • Protected Marine Areas

    Several marine national parks and sanctuaries have been established to protect critical habitats. These areas ensure the survival of endangered species and maintain the delicate balance of marine ecosystems. Bar Reef Marine Sanctuary, Hikkaduwa Marine National Park, and Pigeon Island are prime examples of successful conservation initiatives.

  • Turtle Conservation Projects

    Sri Lanka's coastline is crucial nesting ground for five species of sea turtles. Conservation projects work tirelessly to protect nesting sites, rescue injured turtles, and educate communities about the importance of these ancient mariners. Visitors can participate in turtle watching programs and learn about conservation efforts firsthand.


Experience the Underwater Paradise

  • Diving & Snorkeling

    Sri Lanka offers world-class diving experiences with visibility often exceeding 20 meters. Explore colorful coral gardens, encounter majestic manta rays, and discover fascinating shipwrecks from different eras. The best diving season runs from November to April on the west and south coasts.

  • Whale Watching

    Mirissa and Trincomalee are renowned for blue whale and sperm whale sightings. The deep waters off the southern coast serve as a migration route for these gentle giants. Witness the spectacular sight of the world's largest creatures in their natural habitat.

  • Sustainable Tourism

    When exploring Sri Lanka's marine wonders, choose eco-friendly tour operators who prioritize conservation. Respect marine life by maintaining safe distances, avoiding touching coral reefs, and never feeding wild animals. Your responsible choices help preserve these treasures for future generations.
